Why Stocks Go Up

Why might a stock go up in the long term?

Key terms: Bullish: stock go up. Bearish: stock go down.

Monetary factors

this includes tangible assets such as cash reserves, property, equipment, and investments, which provide a baseline value and reduce the risk of bankruptcy. A company with strong assets is generally more stable and can survive downturns better.

1. earnings. Earnings reports show how much profit a company is generating and whether revenue is increasing or declining. Consistent profits, strong cash flow, and improving margins signal that the business model is working. Over time, companies that reliably earn money tend to attract investors, pushing demand for the stock.

2. dividends (when a company is earning so much that they pay back the extra to shareholders). Companies that return profits to shareholders through dividends are often viewed as financially healthy.

Growth factors

Growth factors relate to the company's future potential. Even if a company is not highly profitable today, investors may value it highly if they believe it will grow significantly.

competitive advantage. This could be a strong brand, proprietary technology, network effects, or high switching costs that make it difficult for competitors to take market share. Companies with consistent advantages are more likely to dominate their industry over time.

innovations. Firms that continuously improve products, expand into new markets, or create new revenue streams are more likely to grow earnings in the future. Similarly, customer loyalty and strong demand indicate that revenue is sustainable and scalable.

market size. A company operating in a growing industry has more room to expand compared to one in a stagnant or shrinking market.

In the long run, a stock goes up when a company strengthens its financial position and/or increases its future earning potential. If investors believe a company will be more profitable, more dominant, or more important in the future than it is today, they are willing to pay a higher price for its stock. This rising demand is what drives long-term growth.
